Charleston, SC

Charleston is the largest city in the U.S. state of South Carolina, the county seat of Charleston County, and the principal city in the Charlestonâ€“North Charleston metropolitan area. Charleston had a population of 150,277 as of the 2020 U.S. Census.

Charleston was founded in 1670 as Charles Town, honoring King Charles II. It remained unincorporated throughout the colonial period; its government was handled directly by a colonial legislature and a governor sent by Parliament.

Charleston's significance in American history is tied to its role as a major slave trading port. In 2018, the city formally apologized for its role in the American Slave trade after CNN noted that slavery "riddles the history" of Charleston.
